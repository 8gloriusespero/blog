Building a USB to RS422 cable for your classic Macintosh

Parts list

I’ve listed out everything that I used on this project on amazon.

  • rs232 to rs422 converter
  • usb to rs232 converter
  • mini din 8 cable - this is enough to build 2 cables!

What to do

insert pic
First, cut your mini din 8 cable in half. That’s right, you only need half of it. Save the other half to make a second cable!

insert pic
Strip the wires back on the half of the din 8 cable that you’re keeping. note that wire colors should be standard across cables here and for our purposes we will be using purple, green, red, and orange.

insert pic
Wire your RS422 to RS232 converter to your mini din 8 cable halve:

  • Purple to T/R+
  • Green to RXD+
  • Red to RXD-
  • Orange to GND

insert pic
Plug the RS422 to RS232 converter into the RS232 to serial convert

insert pic
Plug the contraption you just made into your USB-based machine and your old Macintosh. It should work!
